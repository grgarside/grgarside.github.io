About this report

The purpose of this exam policy is:

  • to ensure the planning and management of exams is conducted efficiently and in the best interest of students.
  • to ensure the operation of an efficient exam system with clear guidelines for all relevant staff.

It is the responsibility of everyone involved in the Academy’s exam processes to read, understand and implement this policy. This exam policy will be reviewed regularly and at least once per year by the Exams Manager and SLT. For the purpose of this policy the term: student denotes all exam candidates or learners, regardless of year group. Exams Office refers to the Academy’s examination office under the leadership of the Examinations Manager.

Exam Responsibilities

Head of Centre

Overall responsibility for the Academy as an exam centre, this primarily will be the Principal/CEO. The Vice Principal has operational day to day responsibility.

  • Organisation of teaching and learning.
  • External validation of courses followed at Key Stage 4/Post 16.
  • Advises on appeals and re-marks.
  • The Head of Centre is responsible for reporting all suspicions or actual incidents of malpractice in line with the JCQ publication Suspected Malpractice in Examinations and Assessments. Copies can be found on the Staff VLE site in the “exams/general documents” folder.

Exams Manager

Manages the administration of public external exams and the analysis of exam results:

  • Advise SLT, subject and class tutors and other relevant support staff on external exam timetables and application procedures as set by the various exam boards.
  • Oversees the production and distribution to staff and students of an annual calendar for all exams in which students will be involved and communicates regularly with staff concerning imminent deadlines and events.
  • Ensures that students and their parents are informed of and understand those aspects of the exam timetable that will affect them.
  • Consults with the Exam Links to ensure that necessary internal assessments are completed on time and in accordance with JCQ guidelines.
  • Provides and confirms detailed data on estimated entries.
  • Receives, checks and stores securely all exam papers and completed scripts.
  • Administers access arrangement and special consideration applications in line the JCQ Access arrangements and special considerations regulations and Guidance relating to students who are eligible for adjustments in examinations.
  • Identifies and manages exam timetable clashes.
  • Accounts for income and expenditure relating to all exam costs/charges.
  • Responsible for performance management and coaching of the exam team.
  • Manages the process for recruitment, training and monitoring of a team of exam invigilators responsible for the invigilation of exams.
  • Prepares and present reports to SLT showing results statistics achieved and comparable data for previous years.
  • Submit students’ internal assessment marks, tracks dispatch and stores materials required by the awarding bodies correctly and on schedule.
  • Arranges for dissemination of exam results and certificates to students and forwards, in consultation with SLT, any appeals/re-mark requests.
  • Maintains systems and processes to support the timely entry of students for their exams.
  • Evaluate effectiveness of exams and invigilation service.

College Leaders

  • Guidance and pastoral oversights of students who are unsure about exam entries or amendments to entries.
  • Involvement in post-results procedures.
  • Ensurance of achievement of coursework deadlines.

Quality Nominee (Edexcel BTEC Programmes only)

The role of the quality Nominee is to oversee the operation of Edexcel BTEC courses across the Academy. This includes

  • liaising with the exams office on student registrations and certification.
  • working with programme area leaders on internal verification, National Standards Sampling and Centre Risk Assessments.
  • ensuring quality and consistency across programme areas.
  • review of policy and practice in line with Edexcel’s requirements and guidelines.
  • ensuring all documents are on the ‘vocational working group’ area of the Academy’s VLE.

Exam Link

Primary role is to act as liaison for all external exam matters such as entries, forecast grades, internal assessments and results between the exams office and their designated departments/subject area.

  • At the start of every academic year notify the exams office of all external qualifications being taught in their college/subject area.
  • Adherence to deadlines as set by the exams office.
  • Accurate completion of exams entry, forecast grade and internal assessment documents as required by the exams office and the awarding bodies.
  • Internal Assessment/Controlled Assessments are with the exams office for despatch to the Awarding Body/Moderator within the designated time frame.
